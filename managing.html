Managing Friedreich's Ataxia

Assembling the right care team is critical for patients with FA

Because FA is multisystemic disease, a broad multidisciplinary care team should be assembled as soon after diagnosis as possible. Each member of the care team can assist in symptom management to help to meet the patient's individual needs.



Discussing healthy eating and physical actividy with patients may help them achieve optimal results


Importance of healthy eating

The recommended diet for most patients with ataxia is similar to what you migth expect for general healthy eating. Please be mindful that between 8% and 32% of patients with FA also have diabetes, and these patients will require dietary guidance not covered here.

  • The goal of diet guidelines for ataxia is not to offer a cure for FA, but rather to reduce the severity of some bothersome symptoms.
  • The benefits of an ataxia diet is sound nutition to support the healthy body weight and normal bodily functions.
  • Ataxia patients may benefit from a diet that restricts simple carbohydrates and one that is high in fiber

    • Along with healthy eating, some patients may banefict from taking micronutrients.


    Role of Physical Activity

    While healthy eating is a great start, patients who include regular exercise in their routine may achieve optimal results. Include a physycal therapist in your patient's care to ensure that they are instructed on exercises specifically tailored to delay the advancement of balance problems.

    The Managing FA Brochure, developed for patients with FA, includes ways to have a healthy, balanced diet as well as recommended exercises patients can try
